Introduction

A well-structured essay is crucial for effectively presenting your arguments. In this module, we'll explore the standard structure of an argumentative essay and learn how to craft compelling introductions, body paragraphs, and conclusions.

Parts of an Argumentative Essay

1. Introduction

The introduction should:

  • Hook the reader with an interesting opening
  • Provide necessary background information
  • Present your thesis statement (main claim)

2. Body Paragraphs

Each body paragraph should:

  • Start with a topic sentence that supports your thesis
  • Present evidence to support your point
  • Explain how the evidence supports your argument
  • Address potential counterarguments
  • Conclude with a transition to the next point

3. Conclusion

The conclusion should:

  • Restate your thesis
  • Summarize your main points
  • Provide a final thought or call to action

The Point-Evidence-Explanation (PEE) Framework

For each main point in your essay:

  • Point: State your argument clearly
  • Evidence: Provide facts, statistics, or examples that support your point
  • Explanation: Analyze how your evidence supports your point and connects to your overall thesis

Language Tips for Argumentative Essays

Useful Phrases

  • For introducing a point: "First and foremost," "Another key point to consider is..."
  • For presenting evidence: "According to," "Research shows that," "As evidenced by..."
  • For explaining: "This demonstrates that," "The significance of this is..."
  • For counterarguments: "Opponents may argue that," "While some believe..."
  • For conclusions: "In conclusion," "To sum up," "The evidence clearly shows..."
