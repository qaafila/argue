The Million-Dollar Decision

Imagine this: You're the CEO of a struggling tech startup. Your company has developed a groundbreaking AI algorithm that could revolutionize healthcare diagnosis. But there's a catch...

Your lead developer, Sarah, claims she discovered a critical flaw in the algorithm just days before the big product launch. She argues that rolling out the product now could lead to misdiagnoses and potentially harm patients. However, your marketing team insists that any delay would bankrupt the company, leaving 100 employees jobless and crushing your dreams of making healthcare more accessible.

Now, you have mere hours to make a decision that could impact millions of lives and the future of your company.

What would you do? Consider these questions:

  • Consider requesting a detailed report from Sarah, including the nature of the flaw, potential impacts, and possible solutions. You might also seek a second opinion from another expert in the field.

  • Create a pros and cons list. Consider the short-term financial risks versus the long-term reputational and ethical risks. Think about the potential harm to patients versus the potential benefits of the technology.

  • Look for potential biases like the sunk cost fallacy (continuing with the launch because of resources already invested) or false dichotomy (assuming the only options are immediate launch or bankruptcy).
